2021 — 2025 · Holidu · Munich

Bidding model steering up to 80% of company revenue.

Owned the Google Ads bidding system at a 750-person company and rebuilt its logic around marginal-profit analysis — doubling model-driven profit while keeping it legible to the marketing team.

Method

The model itself was a hierarchical averaging system in SQL. I ran independent statistical analyses in R — per-product and per-region Gamma and Gaussian GLMs, AIC-based distribution fitting, and bootstrap uncertainty — to find inefficiencies and feed the results back as multiplicative correction factors and adjustable inputs. Every non-bug-fix change was A/B tested before rollout.

Outcome

Doubled model-driven profit from €2.4M (2023) to over €5M (2024), with €25M+ in revenue routed through Google Ads. The performance-marketing team beat company revenue and profit targets 1–3 months early in both 2023 and 2024.

2025 — · Municipal Analytics · Remote

Standardizing messy utility data, then optimizing the rates.

Most of the work in a utility-rate study isn't the math — it's getting every client's billing data into a shape a model can trust. This tool automates that, then optimizes the rates.

Method

At its core it is a rate-calculation engine for water and sewer utilities — cost allocation, reserve targets, bond and capital modeling, meter-ratio scaling. Around it, an AI layer standardizes each client's raw billing exports to the engine's format, adapting to that community's structure and preferences, proofing for missing or inconsistent data, and flagging quality issues (e.g. township vs. village rate-code splits) before they reach the model.

Outcome

A fragile, manual spreadsheet process became repeatable and fast: the engine optimizes rates against each community's financial constraints and returns a clean, standardized study — bill-impact tables, fund-balance projections, and capital plans — with every figure traceable to its inputs. In production for live client engagements.

2020 — 2021 · Senior thesis · Kalamazoo

Poisson and Gamma GLMs that beat the betting markets.

Predicting where eighteen teams finish, from only half a season of data — and checking the answer against the market.

Method

Poisson GLM fitted on first-half goals scored and conceded per team, then used to simulate the remaining matches 10,000 times. A Gamma GLM variant used expected goals (xG) in place of goals.

Outcome

Pearson correlation between simulated and actual final points averaged 0.89; Spearman's ρ on final rankings was 0.88. Both models matched or beat betting-market predictions across the tested seasons.

Stack

R · dplyr · ggplot2 · glm(family = poisson / Gamma)

Role

Sole author. Supervised by Dr. Eric Nordmoe, Kalamazoo College Mathematics.
